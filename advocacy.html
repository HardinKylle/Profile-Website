Digital Literacy for All Filipinos

Empowering communities through technology education in the age of misinformation

SDG 4: Quality Education

Why This Matters

In an era where artificial intelligence can generate convincing deepfakes and misinformation spreads faster than truth, digital literacy is no longer optional—it's essential for democracy itself. The Philippines faces a critical challenge: while internet penetration grows rapidly, understanding of how digital systems work lags behind, leaving our most vulnerable communities, particularly the elderly and economically disadvantaged, susceptible to manipulation.

The consequences extend beyond individual deception. When large segments of our population lack the tools to critically evaluate online information, it affects our collective decision-making, from elections to public health responses. This digital divide isn't just about access to technology—it's about access to truth and the ability to participate meaningfully in our increasingly digital society.

My Commitment

I believe technology education should be a fundamental right, not a privilege. My focus is on bringing practical digital literacy programs to underserved Filipino communities, with special emphasis on helping people understand:

  • How to identify misinformation and AI-generated content
  • Basic digital safety and privacy protection
  • Critical thinking skills for evaluating online sources
  • Understanding how algorithms and social media shape what we see
  • Foundational tech skills that open economic opportunities
