About Me

Hello, I'm Kylle Marius M. Hardin, a fourth-year Computer Science student specializing in Software Engineering at FEU Institute of Technology. My path into programming wasn't straightforward. I came to it without any background in high school, initially drawn by practical considerations about career prospects. What I discovered, however, was something far more meaningful: a genuine love for problem-solving and the deep satisfaction that comes from making things work, especially when I don't initially know how.

I'm particularly drawn to web development, where I find joy in the entire process, from conceptualizing a solution to debugging every detail until it functions just right. Through my college years, one experience stands out: our thesis project, where we developed a system for life-saving disaster response. The challenge of building something that could genuinely help people in critical situations pushed me to grow both technically and as a developer who understands the real-world impact of our work.

Beyond programming, I'm drawn to classical and philosophical literature , with Marcus Aurelius's Meditations as my constant companion. Philosophy and programming share a fundamental connection: both are rooted in logic and reasoning . Just as philosophers examine how humans think and make sense of the world, programmers construct systems through logical frameworks. There's something profound in this: reasoning, perhaps humanity's defining trait, expressed whether through both programming or philosophy.

Looking ahead, I envision starting my career in a software or tech company, immersing myself in the practical realities of the industry, because real-world development is vastly different from academic theory. From there, I aim to work my way toward larger companies, continuously learning and refining my craft. Ultimately, I hope to establish my own venture, applying everything I've learned to build something meaningful on my own terms.
